About

What it is

Simple Tracker is a free nutrition logger that runs in your browser. No account required, nothing stored on any server, no ads. Log meals and track six key macros against your personal daily goals.

How to use it

Set your daily targets in the goal fields at the top of the tracker — tap any number and type to change it. Fill in the entry form with whatever values you have, then hit Add Entry. You only need to fill in the macros you care about. Entries persist automatically. Hit Reset Today to start fresh.

What it tracks
Calories
Total energy intake — the baseline for any nutrition goal.
Protein
Essential for muscle repair and satiety. A common guide is 1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight for active people.
Fat
Needed for hormones, brain function, and absorbing vitamins A, D, E and K.
Carbohydrates
Your body's primary energy source. Whole food sources where possible.
Fiber
Supports digestion and gut health. Most adults benefit from 25–38g per day.
Sugar
Tracks total sugar. The WHO recommends keeping free sugars under 25g/day.
Privacy

All data lives in your browser's local storage. Nothing is transmitted to any server. No accounts, no analytics, no third-party services beyond loading fonts from Google Fonts.

Install

Add Simple Tracker to your home screen or desktop for free — no app store, no download, no account needed. It opens in its own window just like a native app.

Right-click the taskbar icon after installing to pin it to your Start menu or taskbar permanently.

How to open your data
Microsoft Excel Windows / Mac ▼
1
Download the CSV file using the button above.
2
Open Excel and go to File → Open, then find your downloaded file.
3
If Excel asks about import settings, select Comma as the delimiter and click Finish.
4
Your entries are now in a spreadsheet — you can sort, filter, or chart any column.
Tip: Select all columns and click Format as Table for easy filtering by macro.
Google Sheets Any device ▼
1
Download the CSV file.
2
Go to sheets.google.com and create a new blank spreadsheet.
3
Click File → Import, then Upload and select your CSV file.
4
Choose Replace spreadsheet and click Import data.
Google Sheets works on mobile too — great for reviewing your data on your phone.
Apple Numbers iPhone / iPad / Mac ▼
1
Download the CSV file to your device.
2
Find the file in the Files app (usually in Downloads).
3
Tap the file — it will open directly in Numbers automatically.
4
All your entries appear as a table. Tap any column header to sort.
On iPhone, tap the share icon after opening to send the spreadsheet to someone.
JSON file Backup / Developers ▼
1
Download the JSON file using the button above.
2
Open it in any text editor (Notepad, VS Code, TextEdit) to view the raw data.
3
The file contains your entries and your current goals — useful as a backup.
4
To restore: copy the entries array, paste it into your browser console as localStorage.setItem('st_entries', '...').
JSON is the most complete export — it includes both your entries and your goal settings.
What gets exported

The export includes all entries logged today: name, calories, protein, fat, carbs, fiber and sugar for each entry, plus your current daily goal settings. Data from previous days is not stored — Simple Tracker only keeps today's log.

High-protein recipes

Quick meals that hit your macros without overthinking it. Each card shows the approximate macros per serving.

Chicken & Rice Bowl
High protein
~520 kcal 48g protein 52g carbs 9g fat
Ingredients (1 serving): 150g chicken breast · 150g cooked white rice · 1 tbsp soy sauce · ½ tsp garlic powder · 1 tsp olive oil · handful of broccoli

Method: Season chicken with garlic powder and salt. Cook in olive oil 6–7 min per side until cooked through. Slice and serve over rice with soy sauce. Steam broccoli on the side.
Greek Yoghurt Protein Bowl
Quick breakfast
~380 kcal 35g protein 40g carbs 6g fat
Ingredients (1 serving): 250g 0% Greek yoghurt · 40g oats · 1 scoop vanilla protein powder · 1 tbsp honey · handful of berries

Method: Mix yoghurt, oats and protein powder together. Top with berries and a drizzle of honey. Can be prepped the night before — just add the berries in the morning.
Tuna & Avocado Rice Cakes
Low calorie
~290 kcal 28g protein 22g carbs 8g fat
Ingredients (1 serving): 1 can tuna in water (drained) · ½ avocado · 4 rice cakes · squeeze of lemon · salt & pepper

Method: Mash avocado with lemon, salt and pepper. Mix tuna in separately. Spread avocado on rice cakes and top with tuna. Ready in under 5 minutes.
Egg White Omelette
Low carb
~220 kcal 32g protein 4g carbs 7g fat
Ingredients (1 serving): 5 egg whites · 1 whole egg · 50g spinach · 40g feta cheese · ½ tsp olive oil · salt & pepper

Method: Whisk eggs together with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a pan, pour in eggs and cook on medium. Add spinach and feta when almost set, fold in half and serve.
Overnight Oats
Meal prep
~410 kcal 25g protein 55g carbs 8g fat
Ingredients (1 serving): 70g rolled oats · 200ml milk (any) · 1 scoop protein powder · 1 tbsp peanut butter · 1 banana (sliced)

Method: Mix oats, milk and protein powder in a jar or container. Stir well, cover and refrigerate overnight. In the morning, top with peanut butter and banana slices.
Salmon & Sweet Potato
Balanced meal
~540 kcal 42g protein 38g carbs 22g fat
Ingredients (1 serving): 180g salmon fillet · 200g sweet potato · 1 tsp olive oil · lemon · garlic · salt & pepper

Method: Dice sweet potato and roast at 200°C for 20 min with olive oil. Season salmon with garlic, salt and lemon. Pan fry skin-side down 4 min, flip 2 min. Serve together.
